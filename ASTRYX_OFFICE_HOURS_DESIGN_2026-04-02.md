## Problem Statement

Astryx should not be built as "another astrology app."

The real product is a high-trust astrology reading experience that combines deterministic chart computation with a structured interpretation layer that feels personal, premium, and grounded. Kerykeion or its commercial API computes the chart. The app rule layer decides what is safe, what is in scope, and how certainty is represented. GPT-5.4 generates one structured reading from supplied chart facts without inventing placements or certainty.

The wedge is not generic horoscopes. The wedge is: "give me a chart reading that actually feels like mine, explain it clearly, and do not fake certainty when the data is weak."

## Demand Evidence

This session started at pre-product, so there is no first-party demand evidence yet.

The closest external signal is that existing astrology apps already train users to seek:

- deep personal insight and identity explanation
- compatibility analysis
- recurring cycle-based check-ins
- emotionally resonant interpretation, not raw chart math

What the market still leaves open is a product that feels both premium and trustworthy.

The Pattern positions itself around feeling "seen and understood" and foregrounds in-depth insights plus compatibility and connection features. CHANI leans hard into education, explicit methodology, and limitations around time-sensitive chart anchors. That tells you two things:

1. People do want interpretation, not raw astro data.
2. Trust and clarity are part of the product, not legal fine print.

Assignment for real demand evidence before full build-out:

- Put a clickable prototype or concierge MVP in front of 10 astrology-engaged users.
- Charge at least some of them for full reading unlock.
- If nobody pays, the idea is still a concept, not a wedge.

## Status Quo

Current user workaround:

- They use one app for natal chart basics.
- Another app, creator, or TikTok astrologer for interpretation.
- Notes app, screenshots, or DMs for saving insights.
- Generic daily horoscopes that are not really about them.

That is a bad stack. Fragmented, repetitive, and often low-trust.

The market has two common failure modes:

- vibe-heavy but opaque, where the app sounds confident and users cannot tell what is computed vs invented
- information-heavy but emotionally flat, where the app is technically rich but does not feel personal enough to come back to

Astryx should sit in the middle: emotionally engaging, technically disciplined.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

### Target User

Primary target:

- astrology-engaged, design-conscious women 23-38
- already familiar with birth charts, signs, houses, and compatibility language
- uses astrology as reflection, relationship sense-making, and timing support
- wants a product that feels premium and intelligent, not meme-y or spiritually cheap

Why they care:

- they want language for patterns they already feel
- they want personalized insight, not broadcast horoscope content
- they want a relationship between chart facts and explanation they can trust
- they will return if the app helps them think better about self, timing, and relationships

### Highest-Retention Use Case

The sticky loop for this MVP is not "daily horoscope."

The loop is:

1. A powerful first natal reading creates emotional buy-in.
2. The user returns on the same device to re-read, revisit evidence, or unlock the full reading.
3. The product earns the right to introduce deeper paid reading products later.

This MVP is a premium reading experience, not a content feed.

### Strongest Wedge

Launch with:

- one anonymous reading profile
- one premium natal reading
- one anonymous same-device return path
- one clear premium unlock boundary

That is the narrowest version that can both wow on day 1 and justify returning.

## Constraints

- Frontend must use Next.js.
- Astrology calculation must use Kerykeion.
- GPT-5.4 must be the interpretation layer for one-shot reading generation.
- GPT must not calculate astrology or invent chart facts.
- Missing or uncertain birth time must reduce certainty and feature scope.
- Product must avoid medical, legal, financial, or fear-inducing deterministic claims.
- UX must build trust through clear limitations and tasteful disclaimers.
- Frontend must feel premium, mobile-first, modern, and emotionally resonant without cheap mystical tropes.
- Maintainability matters more than demo speed.

## Premises

1. The winning product is not a broad astrology portal, it is a high-trust personal chart companion. Agree/disagree?
2. Early retention should come from re-reading, unlocking, and trust in the result, not from generic content volume. Agree/disagree?
3. Compatibility should not be in launch V1 unless the solo natal experience is already excellent. Agree/disagree?

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Broad Consumer Astrology Suite

Summary:
Ship natal, daily, weekly, compatibility, multiple profiles, chat, notifications, and premium reports right away.

Effort: XL
Risk: High

Pros:

- sounds ambitious
- covers most obvious feature requests
- easier to market with a long feature list

Cons:

- scattered product story
- weak first release quality because too many surfaces
- compatibility and feed features will hide whether natal + chat is actually good
- expensive content and QA burden

Reuses:

- Kerykeion can compute most of the chart data
- GPT-5.4 can generate interpretive copy across many surfaces

### Approach B: Premium Chart Companion

Summary:
Ship a polished natal reading experience with anonymous input, premium preview, and full-reading unlock. Make one flow feel complete before expanding to more workflows.

Effort: M
Risk: Medium

Pros:

- strongest wedge
- easiest to polish deeply
- clear user promise
- best balance of launch speed and retention
- creates a clean rules boundary between chart facts and interpretation

Cons:

- less headline feature breadth
- weaker viral loop than compatibility or social sharing
- requires excellent writing and interaction design to land

Reuses:

- Kerykeion natal outputs
- GPT-5.4 for one-shot interpretation generation
- one shared chart schema for input, computation, and reading

### Approach C: Compatibility-First Relationship App

Summary:
Lead with synastry and relationship readings, then add natal and daily surfaces later.

Effort: L
Risk: High

Pros:

- stronger word-of-mouth potential
- emotionally charged use case
- easy to sell premium reports

Cons:

- doubles onboarding friction
- users need other people's data
- trust burden is higher because relationship readings feel more consequential
- weak if the solo chart experience is not already respected

Reuses:

- Kerykeion synastry/composite features
- same interpretation stack

## Recommended Approach

Choose Approach B.

This is the product.

Do not start with compatibility. Do not start with a horoscope feed. Do not start with a giant feature matrix. Start with the one thing that can feel magical and credible at the same time: "this app understands my chart, explains it cleanly, and lets me keep going deeper."

### V1 Feature Set

Must ship:

- guided onboarding with birth date, place, time precision state, and trust messaging
- natal chart calculation
- structured natal reading surface
- premium preview plus unlock flow
- same-device return flow
- explicit certainty and safety messaging

V1.1, only after the above is good:

- compatibility / synastry
- premium deep-dive report export
- optional account system
- multiple saved profiles

## Frontend Product Design

### Product Positioning

The interface should feel like editorial luxury plus analytical trust.

Not occult merch store.
Not neon zodiac casino.
Not wellness beige soup.

The emotional target is: calm, intimate, precise.

### Visual System

Recommended direction:

- background: warm bone and fog neutrals with deep ink surfaces
- accent: oxidized brass or mineral teal, used sparingly
- typography: elegant serif for high-emotion headlines, disciplined sans for everything functional
- density: spacious on primary reading surfaces, tighter inside detail modules
- illustrations: sparse, diagrammatic, celestial geometry only where it adds structure

Suggested tokens:

- `--bg`: `#F6F2EA`
- `--surface`: `#FFFDF9`
- `--ink`: `#14161A`
- `--muted`: `#6B6F76`
- `--line`: `#DDD6CA`
- `--accent`: `#B58A52`
- `--accent-2`: `#1E4B4E`

Suggested type pairing:

- heading: Instrument Serif or Fraunces
- body/ui: Manrope or General Sans

### App Shell

This MVP should not use an authenticated app shell.

It is a guided single-path reading flow:

- landing
- birth input
- result generation
- preview reading
- unlock
- same-device return

No bottom nav. No dashboard chrome. No "Ask" tab. No profile center in V1.

The UI should feel closer to a private reading and less like a utility dashboard.

### Result Page Information Hierarchy

The result page must have a strict first-screen hierarchy.

Primary:

1. One emotionally specific summary line that makes the user feel accurately seen.
2. A compact trust strip with certainty state and evidence signal.
3. One primary action: continue reading or unlock the full reading.

Secondary:

- one complete preview section
- one chart-derived visual anchor
- one small "how this was generated" explainer link

Tertiary:

- supporting evidence rows
- secondary sections below the fold
- technical chart facts

If everything tries to lead, nothing leads. The first viewport is a composition, not a content dump.

### Reading Surface Design

The reading experience should be editorial longform with embedded factual modules, not a grid of stacked cards.

Result page structure:

```text
[Result Header]
  summary line
  certainty badge + evidence strip
  primary CTA

[Visual Anchor]
  structured chart-derived artifact

[Preview Section]
  one complete emotional/intellectual section

[Locked Continuation]
  visible next section boundary
  calm upgrade ask

[Below the Fold]
  evidence modules
  technical chart references
  precision explainer
```

Visual anchor priority:

1. Structured chart summary artifact derived from the user's actual inputs
2. Simplified chart wheel if it clarifies, not if it decorates
3. Decorative celestial geometry only as background support

Do not let abstract cosmic visuals become the main signal. The first screen should unmistakably read as personal chart interpretation.

Natal reading sections:

- opening summary, one emotionally resonant paragraph or block
- identity section
- emotional patterning section
- relationships section
- work / direction section
- evidence rows attached to each interpretive block

Preview rule:

- free users get one complete preview section
- the next section begins visibly but is locked
- the lock state must feel calm and premium, not punitive

### Mini Design System

Core visual rules:

- two type families max
- serif only for emotional emphasis and key section titles
- sans for controls, metadata, utility copy
- spacing scale should be explicit and reused, not guessed per screen
- radius should be restrained, not bubbly
- borders should do more work than shadows
- shadows, if used, should be soft and rare
- one accent color dominant at a time

Button hierarchy:

- primary button, one per viewport region
- secondary button, low-pressure support action
- text action, links and disclosures only

Surface hierarchy:

- reading surface
- evidence surface
- muted support surface

### V1 Component Primitives

The MVP should standardize these primitives and resist inventing more:

- `precision-badge`
- `evidence-row`
- `trust-strip`
- `locked-preview-block`
- `upgrade-cta`
- `location-match-selector`
- `result-state-banner`
- `same-device-return-panel`

If a new component is proposed, first ask whether one of the above can do the job.

### Interaction State Coverage

```text
FEATURE                  | LOADING | EMPTY | ERROR | SUCCESS | PARTIAL
-------------------------|---------|-------|-------|---------|--------
Birth input              | n/a     | n/a   | field validation + place lookup failure | fields accepted | missing time precision
Location/timezone match  | resolving candidates | no candidates | lookup failed | match confirmed | multiple candidates need confirmation
Generation               | staged progress copy | n/a | generation failed with retry | reading ready | reading ready with reduced precision
Preview reading          | skeleton in final layout | no recoverable reading on device | unavailable reading state | preview rendered | partial reading due to unknown time
Same-device return       | restoring previous reading | no reading found on this device | cookie/state mismatch | reading restored | recoverable draft but not full reading
Unlock                   | payment processing | n/a | payment failed / cancelled | full reading unlocked | unlocked but some precision limits remain
```

### Empty and Error States

Important states to explicitly design:

- no exact birth time, explain what remains available and what is softened
- no location match found, explain how to correct input
- multiple location matches, ask for a confident choice without panic
- no reading found on this device, offer restart and explain same-device limitation
- reading temporarily unavailable, show retry and trust-preserving language

### Loading States

- generation should use staged progress, but phrased warmly rather than mechanically
- preserve final reading layout with skeletons to avoid visual jump
- if load exceeds 2 seconds, explain what is happening in user terms
- loading copy should reinforce care and trust, not sound like a batch job

### Trust Cues

- certainty badge on every major section
- evidence row under every interpretive block
- visible separation between computed facts and interpretation
- profile-level precision state, exact / approximate / unknown
- "how this was generated" explainer link near the top, not buried in settings

### Paywall Strategy

Best conversion shape:

- free: full input flow, generation, first-screen summary, one complete preview section
- paid: full reading continuation, deeper sections, future premium reading products

The paywall should appear after the user completes one emotionally coherent section.

Do not cut off a sentence mid-thought. Do not let the user feel tricked.

### Onboarding UX

### Flow

1. Start page with a short trust/benefit statement
2. Birth date input
3. Birth time input with exact / approximate / unknown framing
4. City and country entry
5. Location/timezone confirmation only when needed
6. Generation state
7. Result preview
8. Upgrade prompt

### Mobile Input Spec

- single-column layout only
- date first, then time, then location
- date and time inputs use device-friendly controls where reliable
- city and country support autocomplete and correction
- primary CTA stays sticky near the bottom without fighting the keyboard
- field labels stay visible, do not rely on placeholder-only inputs
- keyboard avoidance must preserve the active field and CTA

### Precision Handling

This is a core product differentiator and should feel like one.

If birth time is unknown:

- still allow broad interpretation
- visibly soften or lock time-sensitive sections
- explain the limitation in plain language
- offer a low-pressure CTA to improve precision later

If birth time is approximate:

- show approximate badge
- use softer wording in interpretation
- reduce claim specificity around houses and angles

If birth time is exact:

- unlock the full strongest version of the reading
- make the added confidence visible, not just implicit

### Tone

Use calm, exact language:

- "Some parts of a chart depend on minute-level birth time."
- "We can still read meaningful patterns, but a few life-area details may shift."

Not:

- "Your destiny cannot be known without your exact birth time."

That is melodramatic nonsense.

### User Journey Storyboard

```text
STEP | USER DOES                     | USER FEELS                    | DESIGN MUST SUPPORT
-----|--------------------------------|-------------------------------|--------------------
1    | Lands on homepage              | curious, skeptical            | product promise + trust immediately visible
2    | Starts entering birth details  | cautious, slightly vulnerable | concise explanation of why this data matters
3    | Waits for generation           | anticipatory                  | warm staged progress, no dead-air spinner

5    | Hits lock boundary             | interested, defensive         | complete preview section before upgrade ask
6    | Returns on same device later   | hopeful, possibly unsure      | clear continue module or graceful fallback
```

### Mobile Result Reading Spec

- summary and trust strip remain above the fold
- section anchors or a compact section map should appear early in the reading
- sticky mini-context can show section title or precision state while scrolling
- locked continuation appears in a stable position after the first complete section
- longform text blocks should be broken by evidence rows or small factual modules

### Accessibility Baseline

- semantic landmarks on every page
- heading order must reflect reading hierarchy
- 44px minimum touch targets
- strong visible focus states
- WCAG AA contrast minimum
- async states announce changes with `aria-live`
- locked content must be semantically clear to screen readers
- location candidate lists must be keyboard navigable

### Returning User Pattern

If the device has a recoverable reading, the homepage may show a secondary module:

- "Continue your reading"

This must never overpower the primary CTA for first-time users.

### NOT in scope

- chat
- full authenticated app shell
- multi-profile management
- compatibility
- transit feed
- report archive

### What already exists

- strong emotional target: calm, intimate, precise
- color and typography direction
- trust-first framing around precision and evidence
- one complete preview section before upgrade as the conversion principle

## Recommended System Architecture

### Recommendation

Use one production application for V1:

1. Next.js application, frontend plus server routes/application layer

Do not add a separate Python service, Redis, or a monorepo package lattice for this MVP. The point is to validate one anonymous reading flow, not to build a platform.

### Service Boundaries

#### 1. Web App / Application Layer

Tech:

- Next.js App Router
- TypeScript
- Supabase Postgres
- server routes for anonymous session handling
- external chart provider call
- GPT-5.4 structured outputs

Responsibilities:

- anonymous session persistence
- location candidate resolution
- timezone resolution and UTC snapshot generation
- chart computation orchestration
- business rules and certainty logic
- prompt assembly and versioning
- one-shot GPT-5.4 reading generation
- payment/unlock orchestration
- same-device return recovery
- DB-backed idempotency for preview/unlock/return flows

#### 2. External Chart Provider

Tech:

- Kerykeion commercial/API path

Responsibilities:

- deterministic natal chart calculation
- canonical chart metadata
- calculation warnings

The provider is an external dependency, not a separately deployed service in V1.

### Important Licensing Risk

Kerykeion is AGPL-3.0 and explicitly says that closed-source/commercial web-app usage should consider its paid Astrologer API or open-source compatibility.

This is not a footnote. It is a board-level product constraint.

Before implementation, decide one of:

- open-source the relevant code under a compatible license
- use the Kerykeion commercial/API path
- negotiate a commercial arrangement with the maintainer

Do not sleepwalk into production with AGPL confusion.

## High-Level API Surface

### Web App to Application Layer

- `POST /api/reading/start`
- `POST /api/location/resolve`
- `POST /api/reading/generate`
- `GET /api/reading/preview`
- `GET /api/reading/continue`
- `POST /api/unlock/checkout`
- `POST /api/unlock/webhook`
- `GET /api/reading/full`

All provider responses should be deterministic and schema-validated before they become app state.

### Execution Model

- location resolution and timezone conversion happen synchronously on the server
- chart computation happens synchronously for the initial MVP
- GPT reading generation happens once per successful chart snapshot
- subsequent preview, continue, and unlock reads reuse persisted snapshots and readings

No queue. No Redis. No background transit refresh in V1.

## Prompt Strategy and Separation of Responsibilities

### Kerykeion

Responsible for:

- planetary positions
- houses
- aspects
- chart serialization and metadata

Future expansion can use transits and synastry later, but V1 only needs natal chart computation.

Not responsible for:

- user-facing prose
- recommendation copy
- safety policy

### Business Rules Layer

Responsible for:

- canonical schema normalization
- precision and certainty logic
- which facts are surfaced to GPT
- which claims are forbidden when birth time is weak
- safety taxonomy
- reading section definitions
- UI-ready interpretation targets

This layer should transform raw chart output into a concise "fact bundle" with:

- facts
- confidence state
- allowed interpretations
- blocked interpretations
- evidence references

### GPT-5.4

Responsible for:

- natural-language explanation
- generating structured reading blocks
- tone shaping within the allowed fact bundle

Not responsible for:

- chart calculation
- interactive follow-up conversation products
- inferring unsupported placements
- inventing certainty
- making high-stakes predictions

### Recommended Prompt Pattern

Step 1:

- produce structured reading JSON from normalized fact bundle
- use strict response schema
- require citations to fact identifiers

Step 2:

- render that JSON into app reading sections

Step 3:

- persist `outputStructured` as the source of truth
- derive `outputRendered` from `outputStructured`
- do not let rendered prose become the canonical data model

Version everything:

- prompt template version
- model snapshot
- rule version
- calculation config version

## Technical Product Calls

### House System

Pick one house system for V1 and expose it clearly in the trust docs.

Do not let users toggle systems in launch version. That explodes support complexity and undermines trust if interpretations change dramatically without explanation.

### Caching

Performance and reuse strategy:

- persist chart snapshots and readings in the database
- key reuse by session token + input hash + chart snapshot + prompt version + model snapshot
- preview, continue, payment callback, and full reading views must reuse existing rows before attempting regeneration
- keep `rawPayload` cold and out of the main runtime read path

### Evaluation

Build eval sets early:

- exact birth time cases
- approximate birth time cases
- unknown birth time cases
- safety refusal cases
- citation correctness cases

## Risks, Trade-Offs, Failure Modes

### 1. Licensing Risk

The Kerykeion AGPL/commercial boundary is real. Handle it first.

### 2. Crowded Category Risk

Astrology is crowded. If Astryx is merely "nicer copy plus prettier astrology UI," it disappears.

### 3. Trust Failure

If GPT states chart facts the calculation layer did not provide, the product loses its reason to exist.

### 4. Precision Failure

If users with unknown birth time still get angle- or house-heavy copy, the app will feel fake.

### 5. Return Failure

If same-device return is confusing, fragile, or loses the reading, the product will feel disposable.

### 6. Cost Creep

If preview, return, and unlock flows re-trigger GPT or chart computation, margins and waiting time will get ugly fast. Persist once, reuse often.

### 7. Design Failure

If the UI leans into cheap mystical cues, premium trust collapses. The product should feel expensive, not enchanted.

## Success Criteria

Product success for V1:

- user completes onboarding in under 4 minutes
- first reading feels specific enough that users want to continue or unlock
- a meaningful percentage of free users hit the paywall after preview, not before preview
- same-device return successfully restores the reading without surprise or data loss
- support complaints about "this is wrong" are traceable to explicit precision handling, not vague AI errors

Early demand proof:

- 10 target users complete the full flow
- 5 say they would want to keep using it
- 3 pay for premium access or a report
